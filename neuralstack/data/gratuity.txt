Gratuity under the Payment of Gratuity Act, 1972

Gratuity is a statutory benefit payable to an employee who has rendered
continuous service of at least five years, on resignation, retirement, death,
or disablement. For employees covered by the Act, gratuity is calculated as:

    gratuity = last_drawn_monthly_salary * 15/26 * completed_years_of_service

Here "salary" means basic pay plus dearness allowance. The factor 15/26
represents 15 days of wages for each completed year, treating a month as 26
working days. A period of more than six months in the final year is rounded
up to a full year.

The maximum tax-exempt gratuity limit is 20 lakh rupees. Amounts above the
applicable limit are taxable in the employee's hands. The five-year continuous
service condition is waived where service ends due to death or disablement.
