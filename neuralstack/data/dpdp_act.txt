Digital Personal Data Protection (DPDP) Act, 2023 — payroll relevance

The DPDP Act governs the processing of digital personal data in India. For a
payroll system, employee records (salary, bank details, identifiers, family
information) are personal data, and the employer typically acts as a Data
Fiduciary determining the purpose and means of processing.

Key obligations relevant to payroll:

- Processing must rest on a lawful basis, such as consent or a legitimate use
  like employment. Notice must describe what data is collected and why.
- Personal data should be retained only as long as the payroll or statutory
  purpose requires, then erased unless another law mandates retention.
- Reasonable security safeguards must protect payroll data, and personal data
  breaches must be reported to the Data Protection Board and affected persons.
- Employees, as Data Principals, may request access to and correction of their
  data, and may withdraw consent where consent is the basis of processing.
