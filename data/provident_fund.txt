Employees' Provident Fund (EPF) contributions

The Employees' Provident Fund is a retirement savings scheme governed by the
Employees' Provident Funds and Miscellaneous Provisions Act, 1952. Both the
employee and the employer contribute a percentage of the employee's wages
(basic pay plus dearness allowance) each month.

The standard employee contribution is 12 percent of monthly wages. The
employer also contributes 12 percent, but this is split: 8.33 percent is
directed to the Employees' Pension Scheme (EPS), subject to a wage ceiling,
and the remaining 3.67 percent goes to the EPF account.

Contributions and the interest credited on the EPF balance enjoy tax benefits
under prevailing income-tax rules. Withdrawals after continuous service of
five years are generally tax-free, while earlier withdrawals may be taxable.
